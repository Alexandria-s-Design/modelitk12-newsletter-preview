## The Cell Collective Research Lab

Dr. Helikar leads a team of brilliant researchers, graduate students, and undergraduate contributors at UNL's **Helikar Lab**. Together, they've published groundbreaking work on:

### **1. Signal Transduction & Cell Communication**

How do cells "talk" to each other? How do they sense their environment and respond?

The Helikar Lab developed **computational models** that simulate:
- Bacterial chemotaxis (how bacteria move toward food)
- Signal transduction pathways (molecular messaging systems)
- Cellular decision-making processes

**Impact**: Published in _CourseSource_ (2024), these models are now used in **200+ universities** and K-12 classrooms worldwide.

### **2. Gene Regulation & Genetic Switches**

What turns genes on and off? How do cells control which proteins to make?

The team created interactive models of:
- **Lac Operon**: Classic gene regulation system in bacteria
- **Trp Operon**: Amino acid biosynthesis control
- **Prokaryotic gene regulation**: Fundamental mechanisms of life

**Impact**: These models help students understand genetics **at the molecular level**â€”concepts typically reserved for college courses.

### **3. Cancer Biology & Cell Death Pathways**

How do cells decide whether to live or die? What goes wrong in cancer?

Helikar Lab models:
- Apoptosis (programmed cell death)
- Cancer pathway dysregulation
- Therapeutic intervention predictions

**Impact**: University researchers use these models to **predict drug responses** and test hypotheses **before** expensive lab experiments.
